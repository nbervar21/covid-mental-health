What's going on?

Teens and young adults' mental health has been hit harder by Covid-19 than any other age group.

Why is this happening?

Teenagers have a lot of responsibilities. As well as stress passed on to teens from their parents, school, work, and relationships haven't gotten any easier to deal with. For everyone these days, but especially teens, it's important to make an effort to stay healthy, physically and mentally.

What can we do?

Hawaii is rated second in the nation for symptoms of anxiety during the coronavirus pandemic. Every five days, the percentage of young adults (ages 18-29) experiencing symptoms of anxiety has trended upward, and is the highest of any age group. If we work together to help out this age group, we can stop this upward trend. The most important thing you can do is to keep a consistent daily routine, and develop healthy habits. Sleeping enough, and at the same hours every day, is essential to maintaining a good daily routine. You should also be eating three meals a day on a consistent schedule.
